1. **Test Scores and Study Hours:**
   In a study of 50 students, their test scores (out of 100) in a subject were recorded along with the number of hours they studied for the test. The scores ranged from 60 to 95, and the study hours ranged from 5 to 20. Calculate the interquartile range of both the test scores and study hours, and determine if there is any correlation between the two variables.

2. **Income and Education Level:**
   A survey was conducted on a sample of 1000 individuals to study the relationship between income and education level. The income levels were categorized into three groups: low, medium, and high, while education level was categorized as high school, bachelor's degree, and master's degree or higher. Calculate the interquartile range for income and education level separately, and then determine the correlation between the two variables.

3. **Temperature and Ice Cream Sales:**
   Over a period of six months, the average daily temperature (in degrees Celsius) and the daily ice cream sales (in dollars) were recorded for a local ice cream shop. Calculate the interquartile range for both temperature and ice cream sales, and determine if there is a correlation between the two variables.

4. **Customer Satisfaction and Wait Time:**
   A survey was conducted among customers of a restaurant to assess their satisfaction level (on a scale of 1 to 10) and the average wait time (in minutes) for their food orders. Calculate the interquartile range for both satisfaction level and wait time, and investigate if there is any correlation between the two variables.
